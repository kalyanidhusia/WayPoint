# WayPoint — Judge Pitch

> **2-minute judge presentation**  
> Redeploy your skills. Not your life.

---

## 1. WayPoint

### Redeploy your skills. Not your life.

Military experience, translated into an actionable civilian career path.

**Voiceover:** Military professionals should not have to restart their identity or career when they transition. WayPoint helps them redeploy the capability they already earned.

---

## 2. Valuable experience gets lost in translation

Military professionals bring operational, leadership, and safety experience.

Civilian systems often fail to recognize it in career-ready language.

**Voiceover:** The problem is not a lack of capability. It is a translation gap between military experience and the language civilian careers expect.

---

## 3. Meet Marcus Reed

**Synthetic demo profile**

- Army 11B Sergeant
- Warehouse leadership experience
- Little Rock, Arkansas
- Target: Construction / Site Safety Coordinator

**Voiceover:** Our demonstration follows Marcus Reed, a synthetic Army Sergeant in Little Rock moving toward construction and site safety.

---

## 4. One companion for the whole transition

### Translate → Learn → Connect → Roadmap → Apply → Interview

**Voiceover:** WayPoint is a six-stage career companion. It connects the full journey instead of handing the member a single résumé or list of jobs.

---

## 5. The demo turns evidence into action

- Translates Army and warehouse experience into safety-field language
- Builds readiness from a transparent 35% baseline
- Recommends synthetic veteran mentors
- Creates an eight-week roadmap
- Drafts truthful résumé and cover-letter content
- Builds STAR answers from confirmed stories only

**Voiceover:** For Marcus, WayPoint translates experience, shows exactly how learning and actions improve planning readiness, recommends mentors, builds an eight-week route, and prepares truthful application and interview materials.

---

## 6. More than a point solution

**Not a job board.**  
**Not a résumé generator.**  
**Not a generic chatbot.**

WayPoint is an explainable, evidence-backed transition companion.

**Voiceover:** The difference is continuity and evidence. Every step builds on what the member has actually confirmed.

---

## 7. The member stays in control

WayPoint shows:

- why each recommendation was made
- what experience evidence supports it
- the confidence or match level
- where information is self-reported

Members can **approve, edit, or reject** translated skills.

**Voiceover:** The agent never presents itself as automatically authoritative. Marcus can inspect the evidence and approve, edit, or reject every translated capability.

---

## 8. Trust is part of the product

- Synthetic demo data only
- No DD-214 uploads
- No SSNs or classified information
- No fake applications or mentor messages
- Readiness is a planning indicator—not a hiring decision

**Voiceover:** Trust is designed in. The demo avoids sensitive records, never claims to send an application or mentor message, and clearly limits what its readiness score means.

---

## 9. Built for a reliable demonstration

- Next.js + TypeScript
- Deterministic local domain agent
- Browser-local `localStorage` progress
- Static GitHub Pages deployment
- Unit tests and agent evaluations

**Voiceover:** The MVP is a typed Next.js application with a deterministic local agent. It runs without external APIs, keeps progress in the browser, and includes tests and fixed evaluation cases.

---

## 10. Redeploy capability into opportunity

WayPoint helps service members move forward without forcing them to restart their identity or career from zero.

**Voiceover:** The impact is a more confident, more truthful transition—one that recognizes existing capability and turns it into practical next steps.

---

## 11. The path to production

- Verified mentor network
- Real course integrations
- Employer partnerships
- Verified skills passport
- Optional AI-assisted document extraction

**Voiceover:** Next, WayPoint can connect this trusted workflow to verified mentors, live learning, employers, portable skills evidence, and user-controlled document extraction.
